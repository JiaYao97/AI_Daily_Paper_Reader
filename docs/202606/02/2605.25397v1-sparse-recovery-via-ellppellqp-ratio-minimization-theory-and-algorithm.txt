# Sparse Recovery via ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq Ratio Minimization: Theory and Algorithm 

Lang Yu a, Nanjing Huang a, ‚ąó

Abstract 

The constrained ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq ratio model is scale invariant and is therefore attractive for sparse signal recovery. However, its nonconvex, nonsmooth, and fractional structure makes a unified theo-retical and algorithmic analysis challenging for 0 < p ‚Č§ 1 and q > 1. This paper develops a unified framework for this general model, covering deterministic exact recovery, stable recovery for sparse and compressible signals, and convergence analysis of a fractional algorithm. We first establish two deterministic sufficient conditions for exact recovery: a local optimality criterion and a null-space condition ensuring uniform recovery. For the ‚Ąď1/‚Ąď q subfamily, this null-space condition is further converted into high-probability sample-complexity bounds for isotropic sub-Gaussian matrix. We then study noisy recovery. Under the k-sparsity assumption, we improve the RIP-based stable recovery theory by relaxing the required sufficient condition and deriving sharper reconstruction-error bounds. For compressible signals, we establish RIP‚ÄďROP based error estimates whose constants are independent of the ambient dimension, improving prior bounds with explicit dimension-dependent factors [1]. An RIP-only variant is also derived. On the algorithmic side, we propose a prox-linear Dinkelbach framework that directly handles the fractional structure of the constrained problem and prove its convergence. Numerical exper-iments demonstrate that suitable choices of (p, q ) are effective for high-dynamic-range sparse signals and coherent sensing matrices. 

Keywords: Compressed Sensing, Sparse Recovery, ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq Ratio minimization, Exact and Stable Recovery, Restricted Isometry Property, Restricted Orthogonality Property 

1. Introduction 

Compressed sensing (CS) [2, 3] aims to recover a sparse or compressible signal x ‚ąą Rn

from far fewer linear measurements than the ambient dimension. In the standard observation model b = Ax + e, where b ‚ąą Rm is the observation vector, A ‚ąą Rm√ón with m  n is the sensing matrix, and e ‚ąą Rm is a bounded noise vector. The central task is to exploit the sparsity of x and reconstruct it from this underdetermined system. The most direct formulation is ‚Ąď0-minimization, but it is NP-hard in general [4]. A standard convex relaxation replaces the ‚Ąď0 measure by the ‚Ąď1 norm, leading to basis pursuit and its noisy variants [2, 5]. Under conditions such as the null space property (NSP) and the restricted isometry property (RIP), 

‚Ąď1-minimization enjoys exact, stable, and robust recovery guarantees [3, 6]. Nevertheless, the ‚Ąď1

norm is only a convex surrogate for sparsity, and it may produce biased estimates or suboptimal recovery, especially for coherent sensing matrices or high-dynamic-range signals [7]. The main results of [1] can be stated as follows: (i) Theorem 1 in [1] derives a sufficient RIP condition expressed in terms of the RIC őī2k and an associated error bound under the assumption that the true signal is k-sparse; (ii) Corollary 1 in [1] is a special case to the important ‚Ąď1/‚Ąď q model; (iii) Theorem 2 in [1] obtains a stable recovery guarantee by combining RIC and restricted orthogonality constant (ROC) estimates, in which the error bound depends on the dimension of the recovery signal, such a dependence is undesirable in compressed sensing. Thus, the first goal of this paper is to develop some new techniques to obtain an error bound which is independent of the ambient dimension. Sampling complexity is another important aspect in the study of CS. Deterministic condi-tions such as NSP, RIP, ROP, or norm-ratio lower bounds clarify which geometric properties of the sensing matrix ensure recovery, but they do not by themselves specify how many random measurements are sufficient for these properties to hold. We would like to mention that Xu et al. [35] gave the geometric analysis for lower bound of sample complexity of (1a) with p = 1 

and q = 2 , including local optimality, uniform recoverability, and random-matrix guarantees. However, to the best of our knowledge, there is no report on the lower bound of sample com-plexity for (1a). Therefore, our second goal is to give a lower bound of sample complexity for (1a) under some mild conditions. It is well known that how to design algorithms with convergence guarantees for the gener-alized ratio model is an essential issue. Existing methods for ratio-based sparse recovery are 2largely designed for special structures. There have been numerous studies addressing the algo-rithms for solving the ratio models and some special cases of (1), as well as their regularized problem. For example, Zhou [29] solved the ‚Ąďp/‚Ąď 1 minimization by fast iterative shrinkage-thresholding method (FISTA); Zhan et al. [36] combined iterative reweighting, DCA, and bisection to apply weighted ‚Ąďp/‚Ąď q minimization to block k-sparse recovery; Cherni et al. [30] introduced a logarithmic smoothing of the ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq ratio and embedded the smoothed model into a trust-region majorize-minimize framework with convergence to a critical point under the Kurdyka‚ÄďŇĀojasiewicz property; Wang et al. [25] derived a closed-form proximal operator for the ‚Ąď1/‚Ąď ‚ąě regularizer corresponding to (1a) with p = 1 and q = ‚ąě, and developed FISTA-type and ADMM-type algorithms for the associated regularized problem; and further Wang et al. [24] developed an adaptive Dinkelbach-type framework for (1a) with p = 1 and q = 2 , together with a convergence analysis. It is worth mentioning that the work mentioned above relies on convex numerators, special denominators, smoothing devices, or model-specific proximal structures. Thus, the above algorithms can not be directly used to address (1a). The third motivation of this paper is to design an effective algorithm for (1a) by employing the prox-linear Dinkelbach scheme and to establish its convergence. The present paper is thus devoted to the studies of theory and algorithm for (1) under some mild conditons. We obtain some improved recovery conditions and error bounds, and develop an implementable algorithm for (1a). The main contributions of this paper can be summarized as follows: (i) We develop a local optimality criterion for (1a), which shows how the dynamic range of a sparse signal affects local recovery and gives a sufficient condition for uniform exact recovery. For the special case of (1a) with p = 1 , we further derive high-probability sample complexity bounds under isotropic sub-Gaussian measurements. (ii) For the k-sparse setting of (1b), we establish an RIP-based stable recovery guarantee by using several new inequalities. Compared with [1], our result requires a weaker RIP condition and gives a tighter reconstruction error bound. (iii) For compressible signals without the k-sparsity assumption of (1b), we refine the existing RIP‚ÄďROP recovery theory by removing its explicit dependence on the ambient dimension, and further we obtain an RIP-only guarantee by eliminating the ROC condition. (iv) We propose a prox-linear Dinkelbach scheme for (1a) and prove its convergence by com-bining a Dinkelbach-type transformation with a proximal quadratic term to guarantee the descent of the sequence. The rest of this paper is organized as follows. Section 2 recalls the notation and basic concepts used throughout the paper. Section 3 establishes a local optimality condition for the 

‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq model. Section 4 studies uniform exact recovery and derives high-probability guarantees for the ‚Ąď1/‚Ąď q case. Section 5 proves stable recovery under the RIP and the k-sparsity assump-tion, and compares the obtained bounds with existing results. Section 6 gives stable recovery results for compressible signals without the k-sparsity assumption. Section 7 presents the prox-linear Dinkelbach scheme and proves its convergence. Section 8 reports numerical experiments. Finally, Section 9 concludes the work and discusses future work.

6. Stable recovery guarantee without k-sparse assumption 

The recovery results in the preceding section are established under the assumption that the true signal is exactly k-sparse. This assumption is mathematically useful, but it is often too restrictive in applications where the sparsity level is unknown in advance or the signal is only compressible. In such cases, the integer k should be regarded as an analysis parameter rather than the exact number of nonzero entries, and the recovery error should naturally depend on the best k-term approximation tail x‚ąí max( k) as well as the noise level ŌĶ.We therefore develop stable recovery guarantees for the ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq model without imposing the 

k-sparse assumption on the true signal. The starting point is the RIP‚ÄďROP framework of Zhu et al. [1, Theorem 2]. Their result already covers the non-k-sparse setting, but for 1 < q < 2, Zhu et al.‚Äôs proof uses the finite-dimensional estimate ‚ÄĖh‚ÄĖq ‚Č§ n1/q ‚ąí1/2‚ÄĖh‚ÄĖ2 for the full error vector 

h. Our proof avoids this full-vector estimate by applying the same norm relation on localized blocks whose cardinalities are controlled by k and t. This yields a dimension-free RIP‚ÄďROP recovery bound for all q > 1. We then give a related RIP-only formulation by controlling the 20 disjoint-support cross terms through the RIC őīk+t, which provides a condition stated solely in terms of RIP constants.

9. Conclusion and future work 

This paper studied the generalized norm-ratio minimization model ‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq for compressed sensing. We established a local optimality criterion and a uniform exact recovery condition based on a null-space norm-ratio lower bound, and further derived high-probability sampling guarantees for the ‚Ąď1/‚Ąď q case under isotropic sub-Gaussian measurements. For stable recovery, we sharpened the RIP-based analysis of (1b): in the exact k-sparse case, the proposed bound gives a weaker RIP requirement and a smaller reconstruction error estimate, and further, in the compressible case it removes the explicit ambient-dimension dependence in earlier RIP‚ÄďROP estimates and also yields an RIP-only guarantee. These results provide theoretical support for 

‚Ąďpp/‚Ąď pq minimization as both a sparsity-promoting and stable recovery model. We also proposed a prox-linear Dinkelbach scheme for the constrained ratio problem and proved its convergence. Numerical experiments on Gaussian and oversampled DCT sensing ma-trices show that suitable choices of (p, q ) improve empirical recovery performance, especially for high-dynamic-range sparse signals and coherent measurements. Future work includes extending the sampling-complexity theory to the nonconvex range 0 < p < 1, developing convergence-rate results for the Dinkelbach updates, and automatic selection of (p, q ).
