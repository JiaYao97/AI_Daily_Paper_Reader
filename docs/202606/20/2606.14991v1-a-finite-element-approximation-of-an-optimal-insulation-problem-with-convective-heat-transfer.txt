Abstract 

A finite element discretization of an optimal insulation problem with convective heat transfer is considered. The model is formulated as a non-smooth, two-variable convex minimization problem. It accounts for the temperature distribution in a thermally con-ducting body Ω ⊆ Rd, with d ∈ { 2, 3}, and the distribution of a given amount of insulation material on an insulated boundary part Γ I ⊆ ∂Ω. The surface integral over the insulated boundary Γ I is approximated by a mass-lumping quadrature that preserves the structure of the continuous setting and, in particular , yields discrete optimality conditions mirroring their continuous counterparts. Well-posedness, stability, and weak convergence of discrete solutions to the continuous ones are established. Furthermore, a block coordinate descent algorithm for the computation of the discrete solutions is formulated and its linear convergence is derived. Under suitable regularity assumptions, uniform L∞(Γ I )-bounds and a priori error estimates for both the temperature distribution and the distribution of a given amount of insulation material are obtained. Numerical experiments are carried out that confirm the predicted error decay rates and demonstrate the method in a qualitative three-dimensional test on a realistic spacecraft crew module capsule geometry with idealized reentry-heating Robin data. 

Keywords: Optimal insulation; convective heat transfer; Robin boundary condition; finite element method; block coordinate descent; convergence analysis; a priori error analysis

1. Introduction 

Thermal insulation is commonly used to regulate heat exchange between a thermally conducting body and its surrounding environment in applications ranging from buildings and electronic devices to air- and spacecraft. If only a limited amount of insulating material is available, e.g. , due to geometric, weight, or cost constraints, the question of its optimal distribution arises naturally and has attracted considerable attention in both theoretical and applied research ( cf . [13, 16]). While many classical models assume that heat transfer across the boundary of the thermally conducting body is governed by thermal conduction, numerous practical situations are dominated by convective heat transfer, which is commonly modeled by Robin-type boundary conditions . In this setting, we seek the ‘best’ distribution of a given amount of insulating material attached to parts of a thermally conducting body. To this end, we study a convex minimization problem proposed by Della Pietra et al. (cf . [ 15 ]): Let Ω ⊆ Rd, d ∈ { 2, 3}, be a bounded polyhedral Lipschitz domain representing the thermally conducting body , with (topological) boundary ∂Ω decomposed into an insulation part (i.e. , Γ I ) (to which the insulating material is attached) and a Neumann part ( i.e. , Γ N ). 1.1 Main contributions 

The main contributions of the present paper can be summarized as follows: 

1. A finite element discretization of the non-smooth convex minimization problem, given via the minimization of (1.1) , is introduced, in which the insulation boundary integral is approximated by means of a mass-lumping quadrature. This quadrature preserves the structure of the continu-ous problem and yields discrete optimality conditions that closely mirror the continuous setting ;

2. Weak convergence of discrete solutions to a solution of the continuous problem is established by passing to the limit in the discrete optimality conditions; 

3. A block coordinate descent algorithm for the computation of discrete solutions based on the dis-crete optimality system is formulated, and its well-posedness and linear convergence are proved; 

4. Uniform L∞(Γ I )-bounds for both the continuous and discrete temperature distribution and the distribution function are derived using continuous and discrete Stampacchia truncation arguments, providing a key ingredient for the subsequent error analysis; 

5. Under appropriate regularity assumptions, including fractional regularity, a priori error estimates for both the temperature distribution and the distribution function are established; 

6. An H1(Γ I )-stable quasi-interpolation operator and a positivity- and mean-preserving projec-tion are constructed as technical tools required for the error analysis; 

7. Numerical experiments are presented that confirm the optimality of the predicted error decay rates and illustrate the practical applicability of the proposed approach, including a three-dimensional capsule example with an idealized reentry-heating Robin datum. 

1.2 Related contributions 

The existing literature primarily addresses either the theoretical analysis of the minimization of (1.1) or the numerical analysis of related eigenvalue-type problems, typically in the setting 

∂Ω ∈ C1,1 with Γ I = ∂Ω and predominantly for heat transfer governed by conduction: 

• Theoretical analysis: There is a rich literature deriving energy functionals related to (1.1) under conductive heat transfer as asymptotic limits as the thickness of the insulating layer tends to zero (see, e.g. , [ 1] when ∂Ω ∈ C1,1, [ 4] when ∂Ω ∈ C0,1 is piece-wise flat, and [ 14 ] for a recent overview of the literature). The energy functional (1.1) itself was derived in [ 15 ] for ∂Ω ∈ C1,1

and has recently been complemented by the case ∂Ω ∈ C0,1 with piece-wise flat boundary in [ 2]; 

• Numerical analysis: Numerical analyses have been carried out for related eigenvalue problems in [ 6 , 8, 7] and for a related optimal insulation problem under conductive heat transfer in [ 3].
