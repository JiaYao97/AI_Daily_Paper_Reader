# Input-to-State Stable Bundle Koopman Neural ODEs for Learning Controlled Dynamics under Environmental Constraints 

Lin Feng 

Faculty of Engineering, King Saud University, Jeddah, Saudi Arabia. 

Abstract ‚ÄĒWe propose ISS-BKNO, a unified framework that integrates Koopman operator identification, Neural ordinary differential equations (ODEs), fiber bundle geometry, and input-to-state stability (ISS) certification. Unlike prior approaches that address stability, extrinsic inputs, or environmental constraints in isolation, the proposed framework simultaneously learns controlled nonlinear dynamics while guaranteeing global conver-gence and a computable ISS gain. The architecture introduces a three-stage lifting pipeline: a bundle-aware encoder that sep-arates environment-specific fibers, an environment-conditioned Koopman backbone whose matrix spectrum is constrained to lie in the left half-plane, and a residual neural ODE correction whose Jacobian satisfies a quadratic sector bound. Lyapunov-based ISS regularization turns the stability requirement into a differentiable penalty that is jointly optimized with the prediction objective. Theoretical results establish fiber invariance, ISS with an explicit gain formula, and an approximation error bound that scales with the EDMD residual. Experiments on a pendulum, cart-pole, a unicycle-based navigation task, and a Franka Emika manipulator demonstrate substantially improved prediction accuracy and robustness under matched disturbances compared with existing Neural ODE and Koopman baselines. 

Index Terms ‚ÄĒKoopman operator, Neural ODEs, input-to-state stability, fiber bundle, environmental constraints, contraction theory, data-driven control. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

The ability to learn accurate, certifiably stable models of controlled dynamical systems from data is a prerequisite for deploying learning-based controllers in safety-critical settings. Three broad research threads have made significant progress toward this goal, but they have largely advanced independently. The first thread concerns Koopman operator methods [1], [7], [8]. The central idea‚ÄĒthat nonlinear dynamics can be represented as a linear semigroup acting on a lifted observable space‚ÄĒhas been exploited for model predictive control [7] and ISS verification of identified models [14]. The practical al-gorithm for computing Koopman approximations is Extended Dynamic Mode Decomposition (EDMD) [4], introduced as an extension of Dynamic Mode Decomposition [3]; convergence of EDMD to the true Koopman operator as dictionary size grows was established in [6]. Deep encoder‚Äďdecoder architec-tures have further improved lifting quality by jointly learning the observable map from data [9], substantially reducing the approximation residual at the cost of losing the explicit spectral structure that makes EDMD certificates tractable. The second thread concerns stable Neural ODEs . Chen et al. [10] proposed parameterizing the derivative of a hidden state with a neural network and training via the adjoint sensitiv-ity method, enabling continuous-depth models with constant memory cost. A persistent limitation of this representation is the absence of any stability guarantee: the learned vector field can be contracting near training trajectories yet diverge under distributional shift. ControlSynth Neural ODEs [11] address this by introducing an auxiliary sub-network that enforces a Persidskii-type sector inequality on the Jacobian, certifying global convergence through tractable linear matrix inequalities (LMIs) even for complex dynamical regimes. The theoretical certificate derived in [11] shows that systems in this class satisfy a Lyapunov decrease condition with an explicit rate, a property we inherit and extend in the present work. The third thread concerns environment-aware and geometry-constrained learning . When operating conditions vary continuously‚ÄĒroad friction, payload, atmospheric pressure‚ÄĒ a single nominal model cannot generalize reliably. ICODE [12] addresses this by treating the extrinsic input őĺ as an explicit real-time argument of the Neural ODE vector field, deriving sufficient conditions for contraction to hold uniformly over the environment space. At a finer geometric level, [13] introduces a fiber bundle over the state manifold whose structure encodes sensing-dependent constraints and enables measurement-aware Control Barrier Functions (CBFs) that adapt to local observation quality, with provable convergence and safety preservation. Despite these advances, no existing method simultaneously provides: (i) an explicit environment-conditioned latent coor-dinate system that separates fiber geometry from dynamics; (ii) an ISS certificate for the composite Koopman‚ÄďNeural ODE model that is checkable via a semidefinite program; and (iii) a unified training objective that enforces all three properties jointly. This paper provides exactly these missing ingredients through the ISS-BKNO framework.

II. R ELATED WORK 

A. Koopman Operator Identification 

Koopman‚Äôs observation [2] that any measure- preserving dynamical system admits a unitary linear representation on the space of L2 observables motivated decades of spectral analysis. The practical identification algorithms are DMD [3], EDMD [4], and their kernel [5] and deep variants [9]. Stability properties of EDMD-identified models were first characterized in [6], and an LMI-based ISS verification pipeline specifically for Koopman-identified models was developed in [14]‚ÄĒthe latter forms the theoretical starting point for the ISS regularizer in Section V. Koopman-based MPC [7] demonstrated that the linear structure of the lifted model enables efficient receding-horizon optimization. 

B. Stable Neural ODEs and Contraction Theory 

Neural ODEs [10] extended residual networks to continuous depth by replacing the discrete layer stack with an ODE initial-value problem, enabling memory-efficient training via the adjoint method. Convergence-certified variants include stable RNNs via monotone operator theory [18] and ControlSynth Neural ODEs [11], which enforce global convergence through Persidskii-type LMIs derived from contraction analysis [16]. The contraction framework of [16] is central to our stable latent dynamics parameterization: by restricting the symmetric part of K(őĺ) to be negative definite, every trajectory in the fiber contracts toward the nominal solution at an exponential rate. 

C. Environment-Aware Dynamics and Bundle Structures 

ICODE [12] demonstrated that treating environmental vari-ables as explicit inputs to the Neural ODE‚ÄĒrather than absorbing them into the model weights‚ÄĒenables contraction guarantees to hold uniformly over the environment space. This insight directly informs the bilinear structure we adopt in Section IV-B. The measurement-induced bundle approach of [13] provides a rigorous geometric setting for environment-dependent dynamics: the fiber bundle ŌÄ : M ‚Üí E separates the state manifold into environment-specific leaves, and the horizontal connection encodes safe transitions between leaves. We adopt this vocabulary but replace the CBF focus of [13] with an ISS-Koopman objective. 

D. ISS and Safety for Learned Models 

Sontag‚Äôs ISS framework [15] provides a principled measure of robustness: a system is ISS if there exist KL - and K-functions bounding the state norm in terms of the initial condition and the disturbance, respectively. Control Barrier Functions [17] complement ISS by enforcing hard state con-straints, and their combination with ISS for learned models is an active area. The ISS verification LMI of [14] certifies that the L2-gain from disturbance to lifted state is bounded; we use the same structure as a regularization objective rather than a post-hoc check. III. VII. E XPERIMENTS 

A. Setup and Baselines 

We evaluate ISS-BKNO on four tasks: T1 a pendulum with varying rod length ( l ‚ąą [0 .5, 1.5] m); T2 a cart-pole with time-varying cart mass ( mc ‚ąą [0 .5, 2.0] kg); T3 a unicycle navigating a corridor with friction switching; T4 a Franka Emika 7-DOF manipulator with payload uncertainty (0‚Äď1 kg). All tasks involve a measurable environmental parameter őĺ that shifts the effective inertia or friction. Five baselines are compared: NODE (vanilla Neural ODE [10]), Koopman-EDMD (EDMD-identified linear model [4]), 

Koopman+ISS (EDMD with post-hoc ISS verification [14]), 

CSODE (ControlSynth Neural ODE without Koopman lift-ing [11]), and ICODE (extrinsic-input Neural ODE without bundle geometry [12]). Training uses 6000 trajectory segments of length 1 s at 100 Hz; evaluation uses 500 held-out segments. The ob-servable dictionary for EDMD baselines comprises degree-3 Chebyshev polynomials; ISS-BKNO uses p = 32 lifted states with a 3-layer MLP residual.

C. Disturbance Robustness on the Pendulum 

Fig. 2 shows the angle őł trajectory on T1 under a sinusoidal disturbance w(t) = 0 .3 sin(5 t), injected from t = 2 s onward. Methods without an explicit ISS certificate (NODE, Koopman-EDMD) accumulate substantial error after the disturbance onset. CSODE and ICODE recover but with visible oscilla-tions. ISS-BKNO maintains a bounded error envelope whose magnitude matches the theoretical bound (9). 

D. Quantitative Summary 

Table I reports prediction RMSE and the LMI feasibility rate (fraction of test episodes for which the ISS certificate (7) remains feasible) across all four tasks. ISS-BKNO achieves the lowest RMSE on every benchmark and a 100% feasibility rate, confirming that the Hurwitz parameterization (5) prevents the LMI from becoming infeasible during testing. The 94% feasibility rate of Koopman+ISS arises because the unconstrained EDMD regression occasionally produces a matrix AK with spectral radius slightly above 1 under distributional shift, precisely the failure mode that [14] flags as requiring re-identification. The parameterization (5) prevents this entirely by construction. VIII. C ONCLUSION 

ISS-BKNO unifies four complementary ideas‚ÄĒfiber bun-dle geometry [13], environment-conditioned dynamics [12], Koopman lifting with ISS certification [14], and convergence-guaranteed Neural ODEs [11]‚ÄĒinto a single framework with a joint training objective and closed-form stability guarantees. The Hurwitz parameterization (5) eliminates the need for post-hoc LMI feasibility checks, the ISS regularizer drives ő≥‚čÜ to its minimum during optimization, and the fiber bundle encoder enforces geometric consistency across environments. Experimental results on four benchmark tasks confirm a 58% reduction in prediction RMSE over the vanilla Neural ODE and a 100% LMI feasibility rate under distribution shift. Theorem 3 ties prediction accuracy to the EDMD residual őĶN ,providing a principled criterion for dictionary selection. Future work will extend ISS-BKNO to stochastic distur-bances using the i-ISS framework [15], investigate joint con-traction conditions for distributed multi-agent settings along the lines of [16], and explore learning the observable map with convergence guarantees using structured architectures [18].
